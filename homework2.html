Mo:Mo: is a very popular dish in Nepal. It is Nepali mandu. It is said to have originated from Tibet but later was popularized by the Newari community from Kathmandu Valley. This dish can be said one of the favorite dishes of Nepali people.

How Mo:Mo: is made?

There are many ways of making this dish. Every Nepali has its own way of making Mo:Mo:. And my family has also its own way of making. While preparing this dish, we need dough which should be very soft and smooth so it does not burst or have holes while rolling it into roti or adding the fillings. Fillings could be of anything. We can make it with meat or vegetables only. But my personal favorite is pork. But we can add any meat fillings like chicken, buffalo meat, or beef. To make filling we need to add mo: mo spices, ginger, onion, and parsley. But we can add more ingredients according to our liking.

My Family's way of making MO:MO:

First, take flour as much as we need for 3-4 people and make a dough that should be smooth. Then leave it for 2-3 hours inside the fridge wrapped in plastic. Between that time we need to make filling. We are going to make pork's momo so we take minced meat enough for 3-4 people. We put 4 tablespoons of momo masala, and one tablespoon of ginger, add four finely chopped onions, and half finely chopped cabbages, and mix it well. Add salt according to your taste. We can add MSG but it is not compulsory. We take a pan heat 6 tablespoons of oil and add it to the filling. Then mix it well. After the filling is ready, it's time to roll the dough and put the filling inside it to make a beautiful design. The most challenging work while making momo is making design.
